Who is Hatsune Miku and What is the Miku Expo?

Hatsune Miku is a Japanese Virtual Idol created by Crypton Media. Hatsune Miku is special because she is a Vocaloid, which is a singer that is functionally an instrument. Her voice is entirely computer generated, meaning that much like how someone can use a computer to create a song with drums and guitar, artists can use the Hatsune Miku Vocaloid to sing the lyrics for the songs they make!

The Hatsune Miku vocaloid software was released for public use in 2007, and since then over 100,000 songs have been created my tens of thousands of producers. Instantly she became a hit in Japan, with concerts being held in Japan from 2008. As there are no "Official" songs created by the company, these concerts use licencenced songs from many different producers accompanied by 3D generated graphics to show the virtual idol dancing and singing on the stage!
Over time, her popularity grew, and with increasing demand for overseas concerts, the Miku Expo concerts were devised and performed starting in 2014, which along with Hatsune Miku, also includes songs from the 5 other Vocaloids produced by Crypton Media.

These other 5 Vocaloids are
Kaito
Kagamine Len
Kagamine Rin
Luka
Meiko
These 5 Vocaloids along with Hatsune Miku make up the "Crypton Six" who appear together in countless merchandise lines, games and songs together due to their shared producer.
Each member has a unique colour and is created as a relative blank slate, allowing for user's interpretations to be placed on them for different songs or videos. This is evident with Kagamine Len and Kagamine Rin who are portrayed as either brother and sister, or as boyfriend and girlfriend depending on who decides to make media using them as there is no official designation.

Since the first Miku Expo in 2014, there have been 68 in person concerts around the world as visualised below!

Songs Performed at the Expos

There have been a total of 136 different songs performed at the 68 Expos. Each of the songs both range in the producer, how popular they are, as well as which Vocalist sings them.
Even though its the Miku Expo, that doesnt mean the other Vocalists arent allowed to perform. Below is a graph showing the views of every song that has been performed at at least 1 Miku Expo, sorted by year released, where you can click on the Vocalist to see their songs.

Looking at the graph we can see an overwhelming percentage of songs performed by Hatsune Miku herself. It is the Miku Expo after all! In fact the most viewed song performed at Miku Expo is actually called "Miku" by Anamanaguchi, an American Band. But during the expos, which of the Vocaloids actually performs the most songs on average?

Looking to the left we see the answer is obviously also Hatsune Miku! She performs 15+ songs on average per concert, followed veeeery far behind by duets performed by Kagamine Len and Kagamine Rin who perform around 2-3 songs per expo, which is more than the solos they usually perform. All Vocaloids perform at least 1 solo song most Expos, with duets being more of a rarity that appears once in a while.

Above is also a chart depicting the Songs that have been played the most over every expo. As we mentioned before, there have been 68 Expo performances held. Checking the pi chart for the most played, only 1 song has been played in every single Expo!
This song is "Tell your World" by kz. The song is about Miku wanting to connect everyone in the world, which seems fitting for the Expo that connects Miku to the world! Another interesting song is "Remote Control" by JesusP which is the 13th most played song, and the most performed song not sung by Miku, instead being sung by Kagamine Len and Kagamine Rin.

The Producers of the Songs

For every song created using a Vocaloid, there must always be a Producer, creating the instrumental and adding the voice into the songs. As there isnt 1 main Producer that makes songs for the expo, the songs are sourced from many different producers. In fact there have been 93 different producers that have had their songs performed at Miku Expo.
93 Producers creating a list of 136 songs means most Producers only get to have 1 song performed, but visualised on the right are the 22 Producers who have had more than 1 song Performed.
All of these Producers are extremely impactful in the community, with producers such as "ryo" having make "Melt", which has been added to the game Fortnite.
The large amount of Producers really show a wonderful community surrounding Miku and the Crypton Vocaloids!
